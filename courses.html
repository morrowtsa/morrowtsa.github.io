COURSES

Introduction To Engineering and Technology

Instructor: Mr. Pearson

This beginner's course provides students with astounding opportunities to develop fundamental technological literacy and awards them with basic knowledge about different branches of the technological world as they learn about the history, systems, and innovation.

Engineering Concepts

Instructor: Mr. Pearson

Students gather information about separate areas of specialization within engineering and go further in depth with engineering design by applying engineering tools and proper procedures as they complete hands-on instructional activities and explore the field in order to collect experience.

Engineering applications

Instructor: Mr. Pearson

Students learn how to apply their knowledge of engineering design and solve a technological problem. With using the appropriate tools and materials, they will work to make an engineered solution. Along the way, they will learn how to create prototypes and drawings.

Basic Maintenance and Light Repair

Instructor: Mr. Roberson

Students spend time studying and reviewing their exercises with a professional mechanic before they are ready to participate hands on experiments and trials with cars. A few examples of these tests are oil changes and the rotary brake.

Introduction to Digital Technology

Instructor: Ms. Smith

Students to grasp important digital proficiency as well as basic computer knowledge. Scholars face the hands-on dangers of sharing information through the Internet and how to protect themselves from the harsh side of the web. Furthermore, they also learn about the many branches of Internet technology ranging from coding, history, and software.

Web Design

Instructor: Ms. Smith

Students are able to learn the basics behind a website page. Students will learn how to outline a layout, code standard HTML and CSS, and choose a host for launch. They will be able to create a page from scratch and understand how to incorporate add-ons to a website.

Business Technology

Instructor: Ms. Stanford

Students will be able to understand the business aspect of technology, such as effective presentations, management, and skills. Students will be best prepared for the real-world as they will develop basic computer skills in word processing, spreadsheets, and other software and databases..
